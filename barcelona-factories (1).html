La Central del Circ

Circus Arts
Sant Marti
Opened: 2008

This space offers circus professionals a place to safely practice their craft. It includes rehearsal rooms, a multi-purpose room, a materials workshop, performance space, meeting space, and more.

La Escocesa

Visual Arts
Sant Martí
Opened: 2008

This space welcomes visual artists, at various stages of production, to work through their creative process. It emphasizes creative freedom and repurposing analogue technology. It includes visual arts workshop and exhbition rooms, materials workshops, meeting spaces, and more.

Graner

Performing Arts & Dance
Sants-Montjuïc
Opened: 2004

Graner is an old factory complex that hosts body and movement-based creation. It is primarily used for performing arts and dance. It allows for a space for artists to rehearse, train, and perform collaboratively and flexibly.

Fabra i Coats: Fàbrica de Creació

Multidisciplinary
Sant Andreu
Opened: 2008

This space is used for a large variety of creative work, from performing arts to music to visual arts. The goal of the facility is to create work spaces for artists of various disciplines, as well as providing training and support/visibility for artists. This space includes rehearsal rooms, workshop rooms, a performance room, coworking spaces, meeting spaces, and more.

Hangar

Visual & Multimedia Art
Sant Martí
Opened: 1997

This space is a center for artistic creation and experimentation. It is open to artists of all different areas of the visual and multimedia arts, and focuses on providing artists with easier access to resources, spaces to work, and knowledge. It includes rehearsal rooms, a film set, material workshops, a classroom, workshop rooms, and more.

Tantarantana

Performing Arts
El Raval
Opened: 1996

Tantarantana is a performing arts center for theatrical creation and performance. Its purpose is not just to be a performance space, but also allow for the creation of new staged works. This space includes rehearsal rooms, exhibition rooms, meeting space, and more.

Ateneu 9B

Circus
El Raval
Opened: 1996

This space is a community-managed cultural center that offers circus training and performance, alongside other performing arts and community activities. It primarily serves artists working in the circus realm, encouraging community projects and collaboration. This space includes rehearsal rooms, vertical dance wall, training rooms, meeting spaces, workshops, and more.

Sala Beckett

Performing Arts
Sant Martí
Opened: 1989

Sala Beckett is a performing arts center dedicated to theatrical creation and experimentation. It allows for playwrights, actors, and other creative professionals to meet and develop new theatrical works. This space includes rehearsal rooms, performance rooms, meeting spaces, coworking spaces, and more.

Nau Ivanow

Performing Arts
Sant Andreu
Opened: 2010

Nau Ivanow is a performing arts center that focuses on theatrical creation, residencies, and supporting artists through the development process. It promotes collaboration and the exchange of ideas, providing rehearsal and workshop space for theatrical companies. This space includes rehearsal rooms, a performance room, coworking space, meeting space, and more.

La Brossa - Centre de les Arts Lliures

Visual Arts & Performing Arts
Ciutat Vella
Opened: 2011

This space is a center for visual and performing arts, focusing on the crossover between artistic disciplines. This space highlights the work of the poet Joan Brossa, as well as paratheatrical performing arts. The space includes rehearsal rooms, performancce rooms, coworking spaces, meeting space, and more.

La Caldera

Dance
Les Corts
Opened: 2015

La Caldera is a center for artistic creation focused on dance and body movement. It focuses on supporting the creative process and providing a space for dialogue and collaboration. This space includes rehearsal rooms, a performance room, storage space, coworking space, a bar space, and more.

Background & Funding

What Are the Art Factories?

Each facility provides resources and space for artists to work. Currently, the network is made up of 11 Art Factories across Barcelona. The buildings are owned publicly, but they function highly autonomously. Notably, however, Fabra i Coats is run directly by Institut de Cultura de Barcelona. Otherwise, all the Art Factories are run by outside entities within the Barcelona arts industry.

Historical Development

Until the end of the 20th century, private associations and foundations primarily funded cultural and artistic activity in Barcelona. However, after public support for more public service, they began to collaborate with other institutions, branching out and creating cultural and arts centers.

By the end of the 90s, arts centers were facing a lack of resources and space. At this time, they realized that historic factory buildings, often sitting empty, were a great solution to the space issue.

Creation & Growth

In 2007, the Barcelona City Council began the first public measure for the Art Factories. They incorporated already transformed industrial spaces and newly acquired spaces to form the network of Arts Factories today.

Current Funding

As of 2025, the Barcelona City Council is approving more funding for the Art Factories. Up to 60% of the budget of each factory will be funded by the city. The overall annual investment from the city is three million euros and one million in infrastructure for 2026. The council is working to increase the international and city-wide presence of the Art Factories through relationships with art factories around the world and participation in the city's cultural events.

Barcelona Crea Grants

The programs are supplemented by Barcelona Crea grants, which were created in 2020, with the intention of supporting and strengthening the city's cultural, educational, and scientific progress. They support artistic creation, with an emphasis on pre-production. In 2025, a total of 124 grants were given, each for 6,000 euros.

For more details, visit the official Barcelona Art Factories website.
